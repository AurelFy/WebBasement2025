King Smallin

Once upon a time, deep below the foggy peaks, lay a hidden tiny nation of tiny folks named Kaâz-Dûn. Long ago, this place was led by a unity of metal smiths and wise ones. Yet, old tales told of a chosen tiny king — one to unite all clans and ignite lost flame-halls.

Smallin, a young gold-melt lad, had no kin; an old man, Baldik, took him in. He had no coin, no big name. On his neck hung a cut emblem — a clue of lost past. Bold, tough, full of wish to seek unknown lands, Smallin longed to find paths all had left behind. Many found his hopes odd — "too tall a tale" — yet he kept his aim.

At the sun-peak feast of flames, all metal-folk lit up the old glow. Then, all shook. Deep within the holy spot, a black lump did split. In it lay Dakazûn — a tool of god-steel, one no soul could lift. On its side, old glyphs told: “Only kin of Un, the Stone Hand, may lift this tool.”

The bold had no luck. Then Smallin, led by a deep call, held the shaft. Light flew! Heat sang! The tool flew to his palm. All went mute. The tiny king had come.

Yet, evil had come to life. In pits of Udok, a vile being did wake. Odd foes — the Gogaks, shaped by hate and stone — came to seek the tool of might.

The last clash took place in the Deep Glow Hall. With Dakazûn, Smallin led all in a link of axes. A vile soul — once like him, now lost — named Zulda, sought to melt land into a seat of ash. With bold soul, his guild, and the might of old kings in the tool, Smallin won. The old flame lit up anew — no vile touch did stay.
